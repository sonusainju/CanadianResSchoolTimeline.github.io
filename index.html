1620 The first resedential schools was established near Quebec City.



1831 Mohawk Indian Residential School opens in Brantford, Ontario. It will bacome the longest operated residential school, closing in 1969.



1876 First Indian act is enacted and gives government exclusive right to create legislation regarding Indians and Indian lands.



1883  With Davin Reort's recommendations, Sir John A. Macdonald authorizes creation of residential schools in the West Canada.



1884 Amendment to the Indian Act- Traditional Indian ceremonies like potlatches and sun dance are banned.



1892  The federal government and certain christian churches enter into a formal agreement to operate residential schools.



1920  Deputy Superintendant of Indian Affairs, makes residential school attendance compulsory for children between the ages of 7 and 15.



1940s-50s Government makes effort to integrate Aboriginal students into regular schools.



1969 Partnership between government and churches ends, government takes over residential schools and begins the transfer of control to indian bands.



1980s - 90s About a dozen residential schools operated by bands. The last government run closed in 1996, and the last band run in 1998.



1982 The constitution Act is amended and the rights of "Indian, Inuit, and Metis Peoples of Canada" are recognoized.
